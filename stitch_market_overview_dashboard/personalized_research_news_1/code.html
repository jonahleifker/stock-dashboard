Nvidia announces new AI chip architecture promising 4x gains

TR

Reuters Technology

Published 15m ago

SAN FRANCISCO (Reuters) - Nvidia revealed its latest Blackwell GPU architecture at the annual GTC developer conference today, promising massive performance improvements for training large language models that could reshape the artificial intelligence landscape.

The new architecture, named after mathematician David Blackwell, succeeds the wildly popular Hopper architecture that powered the H100 chips, which have become the standard for AI development over the past two years. CEO Jensen Huang showcased the new B200 GPU, claiming it offers up to 30x performance increase for inference tasks compared to its predecessor.

Key Takeaways for Investors

  • check_circle Expected to consolidate Nvidia's 80%+ market share in AI accelerators.
  • check_circle Major cloud providers (AWS, Google, Azure) already committed to deployment.
  • check_circle Gross margins expected to remain above 70% despite higher manufacturing complexity.

"Generative AI is the defining technology of our time," Huang said during the keynote. "Blackwell GPUs are the engine to power this new industrial revolution. We are working with the most dynamic companies in the world to realize the promise of AI for every industry."

Analysts have reacted positively to the announcement, with several firms raising their price targets for NVDA stock immediately following the presentation. The focus on energy efficiency—a growing concern for massive data centers—was highlighted as a key competitive advantage against emerging rivals.
